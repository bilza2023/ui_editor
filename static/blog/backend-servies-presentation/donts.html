Development Rules – Taleem.help

1. Stay Out of Business Design

We build IT systems — not business models. Clients must define their business logic and goals; we handle the implementation.

2. No Unmetered Work

All tasks — including revisions — are formal projects with scope, timelines, and pricing. No open-ended or undocumented changes.

3. Sprint-Based Delivery

Work is delivered in short, clear sprints. Each sprint has defined goals, outcomes, and client feedback cycles.

4. Consultancy is the Main Business

We don’t just code — we plan, audit, and guide. Our consultancy service includes architecture planning, team structuring, scaling advice, and long-term backend strategy for businesses and startups.

5. Be Open to Custom Deals

We support flexible partnerships — for example, a client can sponsor a developer "seat," where we hire and manage a developer on their behalf to work full-time on their project, remotely or embedded.

6. Avoid Feature Creep

Feature creep refers to the continuous addition of new features beyond the original scope, leading to project delays and increased complexity. To maintain focus and efficiency, we adhere strictly to the agreed-upon feature set and discourage mid-project additions without proper evaluation and approval.

7. Testing and Documentation Are Mandatory

In professional software delivery, only about 1/3 of the time goes into writing code — the remaining 2/3 is spent on thorough testing and clear documentation. This is what separates an agency from a freelancer. Testing ensures the product is stable, usable, and safe. We do not allow clients to skip this step to reduce costs — because skipping tests means skipping quality.

8. Documentation = Usability

A system without documentation is like a machine without a manual. For every delivery, we provide documentation to explain how to use the system, where to find things, and how to scale or update it later. This is why agencies charge more — we don't just deliver code, we deliver a complete, understandable product.

9. Modular & Maintainable Code

All code we write is modular, clean, and built to be maintained by other developers in the future. We follow best practices, naming conventions, and organized structure to ensure the project can grow easily — whether you stay with us or move to another team.

10. Iron-Clad Contract / NDA

Every project must begin with a written contract that includes exact deliverables, timelines, payment milestones, and responsibilities. What is not written should never be assumed. This protects both the client and the developers from misunderstandings and sets clear expectations.
