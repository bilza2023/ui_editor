Polynomials and Their Types

What Is a Polynomial?

A polynomial is an algebraic expression made up of terms. Each term is formed by multiplying a constant (called the coefficient) by a variable raised to a whole-number exponent.

General form:

\[ a_n x^n + a_{n-1} x^{n-1} + \ldots + a_1 x + a_0 \]

Where \( a_0, a_1, \ldots, a_n \) are real numbers and \( n \) is the degree.

Examples of valid polynomials:

  • \( 3x^2 + 2x + 1 \)
  • \( x^3 - 5x^2 + 4 \)
  • \( 7 \) (a constant is degree 0)

Not polynomials:

  • \( \frac{1}{x} \) ‚Üí negative exponent (\( x^{-1} \))
  • \( \sqrt{x} \) ‚Üí fractional exponent (\( x^{1/2} \))
  • \( x^{-3} + 4 \) ‚Üí negative power

Types by Number of Terms

  • Monomial: \( 5x^3 \)
  • Binomial: \( x^2 + 3x \)
  • Trinomial: \( x^2 + 3x + 2 \)
  • Polynomial: \( x^4 + x^3 - 2x + 7 \)

Types by Degree

The degree is the highest exponent of the variable. It tells us about the shape and complexity of the expression.
