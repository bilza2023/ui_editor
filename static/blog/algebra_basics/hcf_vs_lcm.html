Difference Between HCF and LCM

Definitions

HCF (Highest Common Factor): The greatest number that divides two or more numbers exactly.

LCM (Lowest Common Multiple): The smallest number that is a multiple of two or more numbers.

Comparison Table

FeatureHCFLCM

Example Walkthrough

Letâ€™s take 12 and 18:

  • Factors of 12: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 12
  • Factors of 18: 1, 2, 3, 6, 9, 18
  • HCF = 6

  • Multiples of 12: 12, 24, 36, 48, ...
  • Multiples of 18: 18, 36, 54, 72, ...
  • LCM = 36

Key Formula

\[ \text{LCM} \times \text{HCF} = a \times b \]

\[ 12 \times 18 = 216 \Rightarrow 36 \times 6 = 216 \]

When to Use?

  • Use HCF when dividing things into equal parts.
  • Use LCM when syncing or finding common lengths, times, or cycles.
