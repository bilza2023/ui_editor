Prime Factorization

What is Prime Factorization?

Prime factorization means writing a number as a product of its prime numbers. Every whole number greater than 1 can be written in exactly one way using prime numbers. This is known as the Fundamental Theorem of Arithmetic.

Steps to Find Prime Factors

  1. Start dividing by the smallest prime number (2).
  2. Keep dividing until you can't anymore, then move to the next prime (3, 5, 7...).
  3. Stop when the quotient becomes 1.

Examples

  • \( 30 = 2 \times 3 \times 5 \)
  • \( 84 = 2 \times 2 \times 3 \times 7 \)

Why It Matters

Prime factorization is used in many areas of mathematics. Here are a few key reasons why it's important:

  • Reducing Fractions: Cancel common prime factors from numerator and denominator.
  • Finding HCF and LCM: HCF uses common prime factors, LCM uses all prime powers.
  • Coprime Check: If two numbers share no common prime factors, they are coprime.
  • Foundation for Algebra: Learning to factor numbers prepares you for factoring polynomials later.

Example:

\[ \frac{84}{30} = \frac{2 \times 2 \times 3 \times 7}{2 \times 3 \times 5} = \frac{14}{5} \]
