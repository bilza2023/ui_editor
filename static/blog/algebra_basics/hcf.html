Highest Common Factor (HCF)

1. HCF of Numbers

The Highest Common Factor (HCF) of two numbers is the largest number that divides both of them exactly.

Steps:

  1. Find the prime factorization of each number
  2. Identify the common factors
  3. Multiply the common factors

Example:

  • \( 12 = 2 \times 2 \times 3 \)
  • \( 18 = 2 \times 3 \times 3 \)
  • HCF = \( 2 \times 3 = 6 \)

2. HCF of Algebraic Expressions

To find the HCF of algebraic expressions, we factor each expression completely and then take the product of the common factors.

Steps:

  1. Factor each polynomial
  2. Find the common factors
  3. Multiply them to get the HCF

Example:

  • \( x^2 - 9 = (x - 3)(x + 3) \)
  • \( x^2 + 6x + 9 = (x + 3)^2 \)
  • HCF = \( x + 3 \)

Why HCF is Useful

We use HCF when simplifying rational expressions. It ensures we cancel correctly and get the simplest form of the expression.

For example, reducing \( \frac{x^2 + 6x + 9}{x^2 - 9} \) depends on knowing that their HCF is \( x + 3 \).
